Selected Publications and Presentations

This paper explores the use of deep learning to predict aortic flow metrics from wearable seismocardiography (SCG) devices, showing that it can accurately identify pathological blood flow changes and differentiate between various heart valve conditions. The study found that deep learning-derived metrics from SCG closely matched those obtained via 4D flow MRI, suggesting that low-cost SCG devices combined with deep learning could supplement or screen for aortic valve diseases.

This study highlights the application of deep learning neural networks to enhance the diagnosis and prognosis of burn injuries using Terahertz time-domain spectroscopy (THz-TDS). By modeling the dielectric permittivity of in vivo porcine skin burns with the double Debye dielectric relaxation theory, the research demonstrates that neural networks can leverage these parameters to automatically classify burn severity and predict wound healing outcomes, significantly improving the accuracy and efficiency of clinical assessments.
